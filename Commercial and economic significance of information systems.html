Commercial and Economic Significance of Information Systems

What is an Information System?

Information systems consist of software, hardware, database and sometimes networks. They are used by individuals and or organisations to help turn large amounts of raw data into useful information for the organisation to use.

They can be different types of information systems, one of these are general purpose information systems such as a database management system (DBMS). A DBMS is a system that makes use of software and data to process data into an efficiently organised and useful format. They are also specialized information systems these system are designed to solve a complex problem or perform complex data analysis e.g. data mining. Another system is management information system (MIS), these are used to produce regular reports for every level of management in an organisation.

Information systems can even be networked to work with other information systems around the world, sharing information and raw data. This is beneficial for large businesses that do businesses all around the world as they can link their systems globally via the internet.

Commercial significance of information systems

Information systems have played a big role in commercial business, large amounts of businesses today utilise information systems every day to help them win the edge on their competitors. And help find advantages and opportunities in the market place. Information systems allow for previously unobtainable information to be extracted from raw data that would have taken a long time or would not have been obtainable by human analysis.

Businesses today can collect large amounts of information on their customers, by using information systems companies are able to better understand trends among there customer base and even on an individual level. They can use this information to target groups or even specific customers with products and marketing.

Information systems are used in commercial infrastructure such as banking and online payment e.g. PayPal, Amazon pay, Apple pay etc. using the internet they are able to perform near instant data transfers e.g. payment funds, personal information etc.

Economic significance of information systems

"Technological advances in processing and communicating information are causing what may be the largest transformation of the economy since the industrial revolution."

Information systems in today's world plays a big role in both online businesses and offline businesses, they are used in many difference cases where large amounts of data is needed to be refined to obtain useful information. This information can be very useful to businesses allowing them to better target customers and get an advantage over competitors.

Businesses use information systems when making economic decisions to try and better predict the outcome and benefits of a particular risk. Also they use them to see how best they can achieve there objectives with little risk/ financial input.
